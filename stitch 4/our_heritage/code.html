The Heart of Barbados

Eighty years of salt bread, sweet bread, and the enduring spirit of Bajan craftsmanship.

A Legacy Written in Dough

In the vibrant heat of 1944, Zephirin's Bakery opened its doors in Bridgetown, not just as a business, but as a promise to the community. What started as a modest wood-fired oven has evolved into a cornerstone of Barbadian heritage.

Our story is one of resilience and flavor. From the early morning hours when the first loaves are pulled from the hearth to the families who have shared our bread for three generations, every crumb carries a history of passion.

Timeline of a Tradition

1944

The First Hearth

The founding of the bakery in Bridgetown, utilizing traditional techniques passed down through generations of Bajan bakers.

1968

The Island Standard

Zephirin's becomes the leading supplier of traditional salt bread across Barbados, defined by our signature crust and airy texture.

1994

Innovation & Expansion

Introduction of modern artisanal pastry techniques while preserving the 50-year-old sourdough starter that defines our flavor profile.

2024

Artisan Future

Celebrating 80 years of craftsmanship with a renewed focus on sustainable, locally-sourced ingredients and community teaching.
